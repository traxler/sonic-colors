Design Rationale

Music Industry Alignment

Our color palette is designed to resonate with the creative energy of the music industry while maintaining the professionalism expected from a SaaS product.

Purple represents creativity and innovation - core values for music creators. Historically associated with royalty and artistry, it positions our brand as premium and visionary.

Electric Blue brings a technological edge, symbolizing the digital transformation of music creation and collaboration.

Amber Gold adds warmth and energy, reflecting the passion and emotion inherent in music production.

Emerald Green provides balance, representing growth, harmony, and successful outcomes for creators.

Technical Considerations

This palette was designed with technical implementation and user experience as top priorities:

  • Accessibility: All colors meet WCAG AA contrast requirements for text readability
  • Adaptability: Works seamlessly in both light and dark modes
  • Consistency: Full ramps provided for consistent theming across components
  • Performance: Color system designed to work with CSS variables for easy theming
  • Integration: Extends GitLab Pajamas UI with minimal overrides

The palette follows a systematic approach with primary, secondary, and neutral colors clearly defined for consistent implementation.

User Experience Strategy

Each color serves specific functions to create intuitive user experiences:

Primary Purple guides users to key actions - CTAs, navigation, and primary workflows. Its vibrancy creates focal points without overwhelming.

Electric Blue indicates interactive elements, active states, and progress indicators. Its calming effect promotes focused work sessions.

Amber Gold highlights premium features, warnings, and value propositions. Used sparingly (≤10% of UI) to maintain impact.

Emerald Green signals success, completion, and positive outcomes to reinforce user achievements.

Neutrals provide clean backgrounds that let content and creative work shine.

Implementation Notes

Primary Color Usage

Replace GitLab's $blue-500 with our $purple-500 in brand variables. Use for primary buttons, navigation, and key brand elements.

Premium Features

Use gold sparingly for premium features and upsell opportunities. Create visual hierarchy without overwhelming the UI.

Interactive Elements

Apply electric blue to active states, progress indicators, and secondary actions. Creates clear affordances for interaction.

Success States

Use emerald green for positive feedback, success messages, and completion indicators. Reinforces positive outcomes.
